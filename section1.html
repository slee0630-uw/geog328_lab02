Origin and History

The Pomeranian originated from the Pomerania region, a historical area between modern-day Germany and Poland. They are descendants of larger Spitz-type sled dogs used in Arctic regions. In the 18th century, the breed became popular in Europe after Queen Victoria of England fell in love with them and bred smaller versions, leading to the toy-sized Pomeranians we know today.
